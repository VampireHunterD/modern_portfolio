About Us

Originally constructed on the corner of Church St and E. Franklin Street by the Reverend Thomas Kyle O.S.A for $500, the red brick building was constructed on 1862. It was used, at that time, as a mission of St.Thomas parish until 1868 when Father Henry Wright became the first pastor of, what is now known as, Nativity of the Blessed Virgin Mary. In 1869 Archbishop James Wood visited the parish and confirmed fifty-four parishoners On May 5, 1873 Archbishop James Wood purchased a second tract of land from Meander Wood for the sum of $1.00. This land was described as "bounded on the north by Franklin Street, on the East by Monroe Street, on the south by land owner John Beatty and on the west by the Catholic Church." In 1881 Father Henry Wright announced his intention to construct a new church on the corner of Monroe and Franklin. The cornerstone was laid on Sunday June 19 th 1881. The Catholic Standard dated November 4 th, 1882 describes the New Catholic Church in Media as " a church that is built in the gothic style of architecture."
